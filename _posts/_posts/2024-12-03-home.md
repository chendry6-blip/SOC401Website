## Executive Summary

Plastic pollution is one of the world's leading contributors to climate change and is a severe problem that needs to be addressed. AI technology is rapidly evolving and is used in many aspects of our lives, from assisting with everyday tasks to influencing policy changes. 

**How can AI-driven waste monitoring help governments identify operational and systematic inefficiencies in recycling systems and design more effective packaging and waste-reduction policies?** 

Developing a statewide implementation of AI tools with vision sorting would be the most effective and impactful way to address this issue.

## The Problem

Plastic pollution exacerbates climate change, poses human health risks through the ingestion of microplastics and chemical exposure, and has significant environmental impacts on wildlife, animal habitats, and ecosystems. 

The OECD reports that, without significant change, greenhouse gas emissions associated with plastic products are expected to **double by 2060**. The World Economic Forum projects that, unless there is intervention, the global plastic industry will account for **20% of total oil consumption and up to 15% of global carbon emissions by 2050**.
